The analysis results are directly impacted by a constraint described in the "Known Properties and Caveats" section of the dataset description.

1. **Constraint:** The dataset description explicitly states that DFT-computed formation energies are based on the PBE functional, which suffers from systematic errors regarding the CO₂ molecule. Specifically, the "Known Properties and Caveats" section notes that the reference energy for CO₂ is approximately -11.17 eV in GGA, and the "Data Generating Process" notes that PBE systematically underestimates band gaps.
2. **How it limits the analysis:** The research plan and analysis results confirm that using the standard PBE-calculated CO₂ energy led to "physically anomalous" results (negative regeneration temperatures), which the researchers had to manually "rectify" by shifting the CO₂ reference energy to -9.0 eV.
3. **Conclusions affected:** The validity of the thermodynamic screening and the resulting regeneration temperatures ($T_{reg}$) are entirely dependent on this empirical correction. Because the dataset itself does not provide a corrected CO₂ reference energy suitable for PBE-calculated solid-state energies, the researchers were forced to introduce an external calibration factor to make the data physically meaningful. This highlights that the raw dataset, as provided, is insufficient for direct thermodynamic carbonation screening without external empirical adjustment.
