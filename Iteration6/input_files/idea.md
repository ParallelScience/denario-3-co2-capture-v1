**Title: Thermochemical Screening of Metal-Oxide Carbonation via Stoichiometric Parsing and Stability Constraints**

**Description:** This study implements a rigorous chemical-system mapping by parsing the `formula` string into atomic fractions to isolate pairs of oxide precursors and carbonate products sharing identical metal-to-oxygen ratios. We calculate the reaction energy $\Delta E = E(M_xCO_3) - E(M_xO_y) - E(CO_2)$ for all stoichiometric pairs, strictly filtering for candidates where both the oxide and carbonate reside within the 0.1 eV/atom threshold of the convex hull to ensure experimental accessibility. By aggregating these $\Delta E$ values across the 1,790 carbonate entries, we construct a thermodynamic landscape that quantifies the carbonation favorability as a function of metal identity (Li, Mg, Ca, Fe, etc.) and oxidation state. We identify "optimal-delta" candidates—materials where the reaction is thermodynamically spontaneous yet reversible—by filtering against `is_stable` descriptors to select for robust, long-term cycling. This approach isolates high-performance sorbent precursors by focusing on the chemical potential of the M-O bond rather than structural proxies, providing a reliable ranking of inorganic materials for industrial CO₂ capture.
