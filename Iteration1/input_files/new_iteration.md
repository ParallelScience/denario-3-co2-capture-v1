- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project has successfully identified a high-potential design space (mixed-metal/polyanionic frameworks) and generated a list of "Design-Ready" candidates. However, the methodological critique identifies specific, high-impact technical weaknesses—namely the reliance on the inaccurate Neumann-Kopp entropy approximation and the overly restrictive stoichiometric mapping—that currently undermine the credibility of the $T_{reg}$ predictions and the comprehensiveness of the candidate list. Because the project has already established a strong "Acceptance Delta" by moving from simple binary oxides to complex, tunable systems, the ROI of refining these specific metrics is high. Implementing the suggested improvements (Debye-based entropy estimation, expanded reaction pathways, and structural stability filtering) will transform the manuscript from a "thermodynamic screening" paper into a robust "materials design" study, significantly increasing its appeal to top-tier materials informatics journals. The effort required to implement these refinements is moderate compared to the potential gain in scientific rigor and the ability to provide actionable, validated guidance for experimentalists.
