- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project has reached a "Borderline" state where the results are computationally interesting but physically suspect due to the reliance on 0 K DFT energies as proxies for high-temperature industrial processes. The "Critical Weaknesses" identified—specifically the misuse of the Dulong-Petit limit and the failure to account for the chemical potential of CO₂ at operating conditions—are fundamental structural flaws that, if left unaddressed, would lead to a rejection in any materials science or chemical engineering journal. 

However, the "Publishability Delta" is high. By shifting the methodology from a crude $T_{reg}$ estimation to a Gibbs Free Energy ($\Delta G$) approach using the Materials Project's `PhaseDiagram` tools, the project moves from a "computational exercise" to a "rigorous thermodynamic screening." The opportunity cost of iterating is low because the infrastructure (the dataset and the stoichiometric parsing logic) is already built. Correcting the thermodynamic basis and incorporating a melting point proxy will transform the paper from a flawed ranking into a robust, high-impact study that provides actionable insights for the CO₂ capture community. The current results are "stable enough" to serve as a baseline, but the proposed changes are necessary to ensure the scientific integrity required for a top-tier venue.
